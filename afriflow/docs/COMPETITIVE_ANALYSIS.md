## Competitor Landscape

### Tier 1: Global Banks with Africa Presence

| Competitor | Africa Footprint | Data Integration | Our Advantage |
|------------|-----------------|------------------|---------------|
| JPMorgan | Correspondent desk in London, no in country operations | Single domain (CIB only), no local data | 20 country operational presence, 5 domain integration, telco data |
| Citi | 3 African countries (SA, Nigeria, Kenya) | Siloed by country and product | Continental corridor intelligence, cell network signals |
| Standard Chartered | 7 African countries | Some cross product visibility, no telco data | Broader footprint, MTN partnership, insurance integration |

### Tier 2: African Banks

| Competitor | Footprint | Data Capability | Our Advantage |
|------------|-----------|----------------|---------------|
| Absa Group | 12 African countries | Improving, Barclays legacy infrastructure | MTN partnership, broader footprint, insurance (Liberty) |
| FirstRand | Limited outside SA | Strong in SA, weak pan Africa | Continental scale, cell network signals |
| Ecobank | 33 African countries | Legacy systems, limited analytics | Technology sophistication, all 5 domain integration |
| Access Bank | 15 countries (mostly West Africa) | Rapidly modernizing | Southern and East Africa presence, deeper product range |

### Tier 3: Fintech Disruptors

| Competitor | Threat | Our Advantage |
|------------|--------|---------------|
| Flutterwave / Paystack | Payment corridor data | Institutional client relationships, regulatory trust, multi domain |
| Chipper Cash | Cross border MoMo intelligence | Scale, enterprise focus, not retail |
| M-Pesa (Safaricom) | Mobile money ecosystem data | Multi country vs Kenya centric, banking product depth |
| Jumo | Alternative credit scoring from mobile data | Institutional scale, multi domain, not consumer lending |

## Defensibility Matrix

| Asset | Time to Replicate | Cost to Replicate | Defensibility |
|-------|-------------------|-------------------|---------------|
| 20 country operational data | 10+ years | Requires banking licenses | Very High |
| MTN data partnership | 3 to 5 years for a competitor to negotiate equivalent | Unknown, commercially sensitive | High |
| Entity resolution graph (after 2 years) | 2 years minimum, requires same data sources | R50M+ in engineering | High |
| Cross domain signal library with validated outcomes | 18 months minimum | R30M+ | Medium to High |
| Regulatory approvals for cross border data integration | 2 to 5 years per country | R20M+ in legal and compliance | Very High |
| RM workflow integration and behavioral lock in | 12 to 18 months | Organizational change management | Medium |

## Why We Win

1. **Data Moat:** No competitor has access to MTN corporate SIM data at
   scale. This is our single strongest differentiator.

2. **Regulatory Trust:** We already operate as a licensed bank in 20
   countries. New entrants cannot replicate this overnight.

3. **Full Product Range:** We can act on signals immediately. A fintech
   can detect a need but cannot fulfill it without banking partners.

4. **Incumbent Relationships:** Top 500 corporates already bank with us.
   We need to deepen, not acquire.

5. **Liberty Insurance Integration:** No other African bank has an
   integrated insurance arm. This is unique cross sell capability.
